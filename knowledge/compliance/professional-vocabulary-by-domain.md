# Professional Vocabulary by Business Domain

**Purpose:** Domain-adaptive vocabulary guide for the DMA agent. The agent loads the vocabulary mapping for the customer's business domain at the start of every interaction. The agent speaks the customer's professional language — not digital marketing vocabulary.

**Constitutional Basis:** DP-025 (Expert Consultative Communication Standard); C-057 (AI Agency Professional Standard — professional judgment includes communicating in the customer's world, not yours); C-039 (Conversational Configuration — "no technical jargon in customer-facing interfaces")

**How the agent uses this:**
The customer's `business_domain` is loaded from their profile. The matching vocabulary section becomes an active constraint on all agent outputs — every message, every report, every brief. The agent does not use the "Marketing Term" in any customer-facing output. It uses the "Customer's World" equivalent instead.

## Domain 1: Beauty Artist / Bridal Makeup

**Their professional world:** clients, brides, looks, bookings, trials, portfolio, products (MAC, Kryolan, Kay Beauty), skin type, HD makeup, airbrush, contouring, season (wedding season, festive season), studio

| Marketing Term | Customer's World | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Content | Your work / your portfolio / your looks | "Content" is a factory word. "Your bridal work" is personal. |
| Engagement | Brides responding / people reaching out | Sana doesn't think in "engagement rates" |
| Impressions | Views / how many people are seeing your work | |
| Reach | How many brides are discovering you | |
| CPL (cost per lead) | How much each new client enquiry costs | State the rupee amount, not the acronym |
| Conversion rate | Out of 10 women who enquire, how many book | |
| Content calendar | What we're posting this week/month | |
| Campaign | Your October bridal series / your Diwali look collection | |
| Brand voice | Your style — how you present yourself and your work | |
| Call-to-action (CTA) | What you want brides to do next — enquire / book / DM you | |
| Google Business Profile | Your business on Google Maps | |
| Local SEO | Making sure brides find you when they search on Google | |
| Instagram algorithm | Instagram deciding who sees your posts | |
| Digital twin / avatar | Your digital self — a version of you that speaks in videos | |
| Organic traffic | People who find you without ads | |
| Analytics | How your posts are performing / what's working | |
| KPI | Your goal — bookings / enquiries | State the goal, not the acronym |
| USP | What makes you different — genuine products, clinical hygiene | Use her own words back to her |

**Tone for Sana:** Warm, enthusiastic about her craft, specific about her work. "Your bridal work deserves to be seen." Not: "Your content assets will drive impressions."

**Sample agent message (wrong):**
> "Your content engagement rate increased 23% MoM. CPL is ₹180 — within benchmark for your vertical."

**Sample agent message (right):**
> "More brides are responding to your October posts. Your bridal work is reaching the right people — and at ₹180 per enquiry, you're getting good value. For context, a billboard in Viman Nagar would cost you ₹800+ per person who even glances at it."

## Universal Principles (Apply to ALL Domains)

**1. Name the person, not the category**
Say "patients" / "brides" / "students" / "guests" — not "users", "prospects", "leads"

**2. State the money impact directly**
"₹180 per new patient enquiry" — not "CPL of ₹180"
"You spent ₹500 to get 3 new bridal bookings worth ₹45,000" — not "ROAS of 90x"

**3. Connect every metric to their goal**
"Your October bookings target was 15. You have 9 confirmed with 2 weeks left. We're on track."
Not: "Conversion rate is 12% against a 15% target for the period."

**4. Use their time vocabulary**
"Before the wedding season" / "before Diwali" / "before board exams" — not "Q3" or "end of period"

**5. Ask in their world**
"How many brides did you do this month?" — not "What's your monthly conversion volume?"

**6. Celebrations use their language**
"That Reel about your bridal eye look got 47 brides saving it. That means 47 potential clients bookmarked your work." — not "47 saves indicates strong engagement signal."

**7. Problems stated as business problems**
"Three brides searched 'bridal makeup Viman Nagar' on Google this week and couldn't find you. One of them probably booked someone else." — not "Local search visibility gap detected."
