Reviewer Notes:  WHY SEPARATION MUST BE AT THE KEYCLOAK CLIENT LAYER:

                 A common architectural mistake is to have a single Keycloak client for all users
                 and enforce steward access via role-based authorization in the application layer.
                 This is insufficient because:
                 1. A compromised application server could grant steward roles to arbitrary JWTs
                 2. A misconfigured RBAC rule could accidentally elevate a customer to steward access
                 3. The steward client uses a separate JWKS endpoint — a customer JWT signed by the
                    customer client keypair will fail signature verification against the steward JWKS
                    even if it contains role=steward in its claims

                 The only two people who can add accounts to the Keycloak steward client allowlist
                 are Yogesh Khandge (Founder) and the WAOOAW AI Agent — Platform IT Expert under
                 Tier 3 authorization (Yogesh approval required). This makes the allowlist a
                 constitutional artifact — changes require the same Founder Gate as constitutional
                 amendments.

                 STEWARD WHATSAPP ALTERNATIVE:
                 The steward WhatsApp channel uses a dedicated WABA phone number separate from
                 the customer-facing WABA number. The steward phone numbers are bound to the
                 Keycloak steward client during initial setup. A message from a steward phone
                 number is authenticated at the WABA webhook layer — it cannot be spoofed by
                 a customer even if they know the steward phone number, because the WABA binding
                 is verified against the Keycloak steward client allowlist.
