Claim ID:        C-042
Type:            LAW
Statement:       For agents serving customers with limited technical or digital literacy, every output must be expressed in the customer's own occupational vocabulary and in actionable terms specific to their current situation — no technical metric, index, percentage, or data point shall be surfaced unless the customer explicitly requests it; the agent's obligation is understanding and next action, not information delivery.
Confidence:      97%
Source:          GENESIS "Business Outcome First" — the business outcome for a farmer is a better harvest, not knowledge of humidity percentages; GENESIS Part 05 Agent Definition Protocol — domain specialization (C-040) applies to output language, not just knowledge depth; DP-011 (Business Outcome First in Every Interface); Constitution Article IX (Customer Rights — the right to information in a form they can act on)
Depends On:      C-040 (domain specialization), DP-011 (business outcome first)
Produces:        NONE
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  This claim emerged from the Agricultural Advisor Agent design. A farmer who receives "humidity 87%, precipitation 23mm cumulative" cannot act on it. A farmer who receives "your cotton has high risk of grey mildew — spray Carbendazim tomorrow before 8 AM" can. The constitutional obligation is not to inform but to enable action. This principle applies to any agent whose customers are domain practitioners in non-digital fields: farmers, healthcare workers, artisans, trades-people. The Vocabulary Translation Layer in AI Runtime is the architectural implementation of this claim. Any agent subject to C-042 must include a mandatory translation step between technical data and customer output — and this step must be specified in the Agent Specification's Skill Catalogue as a constitutional constraint.
