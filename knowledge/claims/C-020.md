Claim ID:        C-020
Type:            HYPOTHESIS
Statement:       In any profession where autonomous actions are irreversible within seconds, human override must be guaranteed to halt operations within an absolute time bound measured in hundreds of milliseconds; relative latency guarantees ("fast as possible") are constitutionally insufficient.
Confidence:      90%
Source:          PRECEDENTS.md CD-015 (under deliberation), Case 003, C-019 (empirical base)
Depends On:      C-001 (human override absolute — this hypothesis defines what "absolute" means in high-velocity contexts), C-019 (empirical support)
Produces:        C-022 (Emergency Stop ≤250ms architectural guarantee)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          DRAFT
Reviewer Notes:  HYPOTHESIS at 90% confidence. The argument: if an action is irreversible before a "fast as possible" halt arrives, then the halt was not architecturally guaranteed. "Absolute" in Article XI means guaranteed in advance, not achieved on average. For the trading context (Case 003), 250ms was determined to be the relevant bound: within 250ms, market movements are small enough that a halt has meaningful protective value. This specific number (250ms) is a GENESIS engineering parameter derived from this constitutional hypothesis — it is not itself constitutional law.
