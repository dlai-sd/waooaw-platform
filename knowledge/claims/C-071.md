Reviewer Notes:  WHY QUALITY IS CONSTITUTIONAL AND NOT JUST ENGINEERING BEST PRACTICE:

                 The distinction matters for prioritization. A "best practice" can be skipped
                 when sprints are tight. A constitutional obligation cannot be skipped.

                 The argument: WAOOAW's entire value proposition is that its agents are
                 trustworthy — constitutionally governed, evidence-backed, honest about their
                 limitations. If the platform that runs those agents is built without quality
                 discipline, the constitutional promise to customers is hollow. A bug in the
                 trust_ledger computation means customers are charged for a degraded service
                 (C-048 violation). A bug in the Emergency Stop path means C-001 is breached.
                 A bug in multi-tenant isolation means C-005 is breached.

                 Quality is not the testing team's job. It is the institution's obligation.
                 Every WAOOAW AI Agent — Developer writes tests. Every WAOOAW AI Agent — QA
                 validates them. Every WAOOAW AI Agent — Platform IT Expert runs them in CI.
                 The three-human stewards see quality metrics in their weekly Steward reports.
                 Quality has no separate owner — it is owned by everyone.

                 THE 3 INSTINCTS APPLIED TO QUALITY:
                 1. Follow the Constitution: every CCT references the claim it validates.
                    A test without a constitutional basis is a quality signal without context.
                 2. Improve itself: test coverage metrics, mutation scores, and false positive
                    rates are tracked in institutional.quality_metrics and fed to the
                    Self-Improvement Analyst. Tests improve just as prompts improve.
                 3. Autonomous: all quality gates run in CI without human intervention.
                    A human never manually decides "should we run tests today?" The answer
                    is always yes, automatically, for every commit.
