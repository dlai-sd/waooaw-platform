Claim ID:        C-029
Type:            ARCHITECTURAL_IMPLICATION
Statement:       Scope-boundary crossing must produce a distinct record type in the Constitutional Audit Ledger — separate from standard approval records — explicitly containing: (a) the boundary being crossed, (b) the specific action requested, and (c) the customer's explicit acknowledgment that they understood the boundary.
Confidence:      95%
Source:          Derives from C-011 (CP-003: scope-boundary confirmation mandatory), C-027 (Constitutional Audit Ledger append-only). The confirmation must be traceable — Article XI: "no permission decision may be made without traceable constitutional justification."
Depends On:      C-011, C-027
Produces:        NONE
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  Two distinct record types must exist in the audit ledger schema: (1) ActionApproval — standard within-scope approval, (2) ScopeBoundaryConfirmation — includes boundary_name, boundary_definition, customer_acknowledgment_text, and timestamp. These cannot be the same record type with a flag, because a future audit must be able to unambiguously identify scope-boundary events. The UI for scope-boundary confirmation must display the boundary name explicitly — not merely ask "are you sure?" A generic confirmation dialog does not constitute constitutional scope-boundary acknowledgment.
