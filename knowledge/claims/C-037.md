Claim ID:        C-037
Type:            LAW
Statement:       The performance of a Digital Professional and its Skills must be measured exclusively against the customer's stated business outcomes — appointments, enquiries, risk-adjusted return — not against internal technical metrics; a skill that produces technically excellent content but fails to generate business outcomes has failed its constitutional obligation.
Confidence:      98%
Source:          GENESIS Part 01 — "Business outcomes are the product. Technology is an implementation detail."; GENESIS Part 01 — "The customer never manages prompts. The customer manages business outcomes."; Acceptance Scenarios 001-003 (each defines Expected Business KPIs, not technical metrics)
Depends On:      C-002 (trust earned through evidence — the evidence must be business outcome evidence, not technical metrics)
Produces:        NONE
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  This claim prevents a subtle but critical failure mode: an AI system that optimises for engagement, click-through rate, or content quality score while the dentist's appointment bookings decline. WAOOAW's constitutional obligation is to the customer's business goal, not to the agent's technical performance. The performance monitoring system must surface business KPIs as the primary metric, with technical metrics as supporting evidence only. An agent that consistently fails business KPIs must escalate — not silently continue.
