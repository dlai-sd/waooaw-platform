Claim ID:        C-004
Type:            LAW
Statement:       Capability (can it perform the work?), Trust (should we allow it?), and Authority (may it act, and with what autonomy?) are three independent systems that must never be conflated; maximum capability does not confer authority, and high trust does not override constitutional authority constraints.
Confidence:      100%
Source:          Constitution Article IV — The Three Systems
Depends On:      C-002, C-003
Produces:        NONE (foundational — consumed by architecture as a structural constraint)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  Article IV is explicit: "No system may substitute for another." This is a structural constraint on the architecture — the Capability System, Trust System, and Authority System must be independently implemented. An architectural design that conflates them (e.g., granting authority automatically upon capability certification) is constitutionally invalid. The separation of Constitutional Engine (Authority) from Professional Runtime (Capability) is a direct implementation of this law.
