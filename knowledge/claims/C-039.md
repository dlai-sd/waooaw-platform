Claim ID:        C-039
Type:            CONFIRMED
Statement:       Agent configuration — including goal-setting, credential provision, skill scheduling, and Decision Space refinement — must be achievable through natural language conversation with the agent; structured form interfaces may exist as a secondary option but must never be the only path.
Confidence:      90%
Source:          GENESIS Part 01 — "The customer never manages prompts. The customer manages business outcomes." (implies the interface abstracts technical configuration into business language); Acceptance Scenario 001 — Dr. Mehta configures marketing goals, not technical parameters; Acceptance Scenario 002 — Sana defines her aesthetic voice through examples and dialogue, not through a dropdown; Acceptance Scenario 003 — Rahul sets risk parameters through conversation about his trading strategy
Depends On:      C-030 (Decision Space as primitive — configuration is the creation of the Decision Space boundary)
Produces:        NONE
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  A dentist configuring a digital marketing agent should not encounter fields like "authorized_actions[0].actionType = INSTAGRAM_POST". She should say: "Post on Instagram every Tuesday and Thursday with educational dental content." The agent translates this into its Decision Space. Conversational configuration is not a UX preference — it is a constitutional obligation because the customer is a business owner, not a developer. Any interface that requires technical literacy to configure a professional engagement has violated the principle that the customer hires business capability, not technology.
