Claim ID:        C-011
Type:            CONFIRMED
Statement:       When a professional is asked to act at or beyond the boundary of its licensed scope, a mandatory scope-boundary confirmation flow is required — simple approval without explicit boundary acknowledgment is constitutionally invalid for out-of-scope actions.
Confidence:      95%
Source:          PRECEDENTS.md CP-003 (ratified, re-affirmed in Case 002), Constitution Article IX (Customer Rights — right to transparency), Article X (Professional Duties — must be transparent)
Depends On:      C-001 (human override — scope-boundary confirmation is a form of informed override), C-003 (authority licensing — scope boundary IS the authority limit)
Produces:        C-026 (scope-boundary confirmation produces a distinct audit record)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  CP-003 is ratified and was re-affirmed in Case 002. This creates a two-tier approval model in the architecture: standard approval (within scope) and scope-boundary confirmation (at or beyond scope limit). The confirmation must explicitly name the boundary being crossed and record the customer's acknowledgment. A standard approval UI cannot double as a scope-boundary confirmation UI — they are constitutionally distinct interactions.
