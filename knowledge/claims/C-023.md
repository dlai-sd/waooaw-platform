Claim ID:        C-023
Type:            ARCHITECTURAL_IMPLICATION
Statement:       The Constitutional Engine must record constitutional evidence as an atomic, durable operation before returning a success response to the calling service — no action that requires constitutional evidence may return success until that evidence is confirmed written to the Constitutional Audit Ledger.
Confidence:      97%
Source:          Derives from C-002 (First Law: trust through observable evidence), C-007 (ledger immutability), C-011 (every permission decision traceable). Implemented in ADR-001 (gRPC for Constitutional Engine) as the Evidence First principle.
Depends On:      C-002, C-007, C-027
Produces:        NONE (this IS the architectural implementation target)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  This is the architectural consequence of the First Law applied to the Constitutional Engine. If evidence can be lost (because the service crashes between action completion and evidence write), then trust cannot be earned — there is no observable record. The gRPC protocol between Business Platform / Professional Runtime and the Constitutional Engine must be synchronous, and the evidence write must be part of the same transaction scope as the action authorization. ADR-001 ratified gRPC as the protocol; this claim is the constitutional justification.
