Claim ID:        C-060
Type:            PLATFORM_INTEGRITY + PRIVACY
Statement:       When the beneficiary of an agent's service is a minor (person under 18 years of age), the platform must apply the highest tier of data protection: no collection of biometric or visual data (including camera video); no storage of personally identifiable student performance data beyond what the employing parent explicitly authorizes; no communication directly to the minor about subscription, billing, or commercial terms; and the parent retains unconditional Emergency Stop authority over all agent sessions at all times.
Confidence:      99%
Source:          Derives from C-001 (Human Override — parent is the human override authority for minor's sessions), C-048 (Information Non-Exploitation — minors are the most protected category), DPDPA India 2023 Section 9 (parental consent mandatory for processing personal data of children), UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (Article 16 — right to privacy), C-023 (Evidence First — all data collected about a minor student must be constitutionally recorded with its purpose).
Depends On:      C-001, C-023, C-048, C-049
Produces:        Private Tutor Agent design constraints, Minor Data Handling Standard (Section 3.0 of private-tutor-agent.md), Learning Intelligence architecture (interaction-signal-only, no camera)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED — 2026-07-13 (Founder authorization)
Reviewer Notes:  WAOOAW's Private Tutor Agent serves students from Class 5 (age ~10) to Class 10 
                 (age ~15). This age range is the most sensitive category under DPDPA India 2023,
                 which defines "child" as under 18 and requires verifiable parental consent for all
                 personal data processing.

                 Four absolute prohibitions under C-060:

                 1. NO CAMERA/BIOMETRIC DATA
                    The Learning Intelligence system MUST infer engagement from interaction signals
                    (response latency, answer patterns, message length) — never from camera, 
                    microphone analysis of emotional tone, or any biometric signal.
                    Rationale: camera data of minors requires highest consent tier; real-time 
                    emotional analysis of children crosses into psychological profiling.

                 2. NO COMMERCIAL COMMUNICATION TO MINOR
                    The agent may never mention subscription cost, renewal, billing, or commercial 
                    terms to the student. All commercial communication goes to the parent portal only.
                    Rationale: marketing/commercial pressure on minors is prohibited under 
                    India's Advertising Standards Council guidelines and DPDPA.

                 3. PARENT EMERGENCY STOP IS ABSOLUTE AND UNCONDITIONAL
                    Parent can pause or terminate all sessions immediately via portal.
                    Unlike adult Emergency Stop (250ms guarantee), the minor Emergency Stop 
                    must also terminate any in-progress whiteboard session and voice streaming 
                    within 250ms — with no "save in progress" delay.

                 4. DATA MINIMIZATION FOR STUDENT
                    Only data necessary for the student's learning may be collected:
                      PERMITTED: session performance, quiz scores, topic progress, 
                                 engagement signals (interaction-based only)
                      PROHIBITED: emotional state inferences, social/behavioral profiling 
                                  beyond academic context, data sharing with third parties 
                                  under any circumstances
                    Parent may request full data deletion at any time — platform must comply
                    within 72 hours (DPDPA erasure right).

                 Constitutional boundary: C-060 applies only when the end-beneficiary is a minor.
                 The parent-as-employer relationship does not make the parent the "user" for 
                 data protection purposes — the student's data belongs to the student (and their 
                 parent as guardian), not to the hiring institution.
