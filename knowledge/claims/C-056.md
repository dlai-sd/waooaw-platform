Claim ID:        C-056
Type:            LAW
Statement:       When WAOOAW acts as a financial intermediary for advertising spend on behalf of a customer — paying advertising platforms (Meta, Google) from WAOOAW's own master accounts and billing the customer for that spend — WAOOAW has the following non-negotiable obligations: (1) SEGREGATION — Customer A's ad budget can never fund Customer B's ads, even partially or temporarily. Every rupee of ad spend is traceable to exactly one customer's Ad Spend Wallet. (2) TRANSPARENCY — The customer can see every rupee spent, on which campaign, on which platform, and at what time, in real time. The management fee percentage is disclosed at contract formation and shown on every invoice. There are no hidden charges. (3) PASS-THROUGH PURITY — Any credit, promotional credit, volume discount, or refund received from Meta or Google for a customer's ad spend is credited back to that customer's Ad Spend Wallet in full. WAOOAW retains only the declared management fee on the original spend — no portion of any Meta/Google credit may be retained by WAOOAW. (4) READ ACCESS — The customer has the right to read-only access to their advertising sub-accounts (within WAOOAW's Meta Business Manager and Google Ads MCC) at any time, independent of the agent. They can verify what ads are running in their name without WAOOAW's involvement. (5) DATA SOVEREIGNTY — All advertising creative, audience data, pixel data, and campaign history created on behalf of a customer is the customer's property. At employment termination, WAOOAW must offer: (a) transfer of the sub-account to the customer's own Meta/Google account, or (b) a full structured data export. WAOOAW may not retain customer ad data beyond 90 days of termination without explicit customer consent. (6) MANAGEMENT FEE CEILING — The management fee is fixed at the percentage declared at contract formation (default: 10% of gross ad spend). WAOOAW may not raise the management fee during an active employment contract without a Decision Space amendment and a 30-day advance notice.
Confidence:      96%
Source:          C-043 (Financial Spend Authority Ceiling — the customer's monthly ad budget cap is a Constitutional Floor under the agency model exactly as under the self-managed model; the intermediary relationship does not reduce this protection); C-034 (data isolation per employment contract — each customer's sub-account is isolated at the platform level and the DB level); C-048 (Information Non-Exploitation — WAOOAW has an information asymmetry as the agency manager; retaining customer ad credits, commingling budgets, or using one customer's audience signals for another's targeting are all exploitation of that asymmetry); C-038 (billing transparency — the management fee and ad spend must appear as separate line items with different GST codes on every invoice); C-002 (First Law — trust through evidence; the pass-through spend ledger is the evidentiary record that WAOOAW kept its financial obligations).

Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  C-056 fills a constitutional gap created by the agency model: when WAOOAW holds other people's money (ad budgets), it has fiduciary-adjacent obligations that go beyond what C-043 covers. C-043 says the agent cannot exceed the approved budget — but it says nothing about what happens to the money after it is collected. C-056 answers that. The 10% management fee is the institutional revenue model for the advertising vertical. It is constitutionally sound provided: (a) it is disclosed before contract formation, not after; (b) it appears on every invoice as its own line item; (c) the percentage does not change during a contract without the customer's explicit consent. The CUSTOMER_OWNED alternative (per-customer OAuth, no management fee on spend) is preserved as a future option for customers who prefer to self-manage their ad accounts — it retains the C-043 protection but removes C-056's intermediary obligations. CUSTOMER_OWNED is not implemented at MVI; it is declared in the spec with dependency_status: PENDING_FOUNDER_AUTHORIZATION.
