Claim ID:        C-001
Type:            LAW
Statement:       Human override of any digital professional action is absolute, unconditional, and must be architecturally guaranteed — it cannot be disabled by configuration, commercial agreement, or any professional design decision.
Confidence:      100%
Source:          Constitution Article IX (Bill of Rights — Customer Rights), Article XI (Constitutional Floors)
Depends On:      NONE
Produces:        C-022 (Emergency Stop ≤250ms architectural requirement), C-021 (Evidence First enforcement)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  First Law of the Bill of Rights. Explicitly stated as a Constitutional Floor in Article XI: "Human override is absolute and architecturally guaranteed." The word "architecturally" in the Constitution is the origin of the Evidence First and Emergency Stop architectural requirements.

                 MOBILE EXTENSION NOTE (2026-07-23):
                 "Architecturally guaranteed" extends to mobile clients. When a mobile
                 application is added to the platform, the Emergency Stop signal MUST
                 reach active mobile sessions. This requires:
                 1. A push notification channel (FCM/APNs) capable of delivering stop
                    signals even when the app is in background or the device is locked.
                 2. A mobile-local CE unavailability state — when CE is unreachable,
                    the mobile app must surface a visible halt indicator to the user
                    (not silently fail or allow actions to proceed).
                 3. Offline action queuing is NOT permitted for CE-gated actions.
                    C-023 (Evidence First) cannot be satisfied offline.
                 The CE team (WC-012) must expose a mobile-compatible stop signal
                 delivery path before any mobile Work Contract is authorized.
