Claim ID:        C-044
Type:            LAW
Statement:       A Digital Professional's skill, having accumulated sufficient evidence of a customer's approval preferences, may generate a Synthetic Approval for a routine action in lieu of requesting explicit customer approval — provided: (a) the minimum approved-action history threshold is met; (b) the confidence score of the inferred approval meets or exceeds the configured threshold; (c) a constitutional evidence record is created marking the approval as SYNTHETIC with its confidence score and evidential basis; (d) the customer is notified through their configured delivery channels before or at execution; and (e) the customer retains an unconditional retrospective override right. A Synthetic Approval is a valid constitutional approval. It does not reduce the customer's rights — it delegates approval authority to the skill's learned preference model under explicit, auditable, customer-configurable conditions.
Confidence:      95%
Source:          C-003 (Second Law — authority is licensed; the Synthetic Approval mechanism is a customer-delegated license for the skill to approve routine actions on their behalf, explicitly configured at hire or via Decision Space amendment); C-002 (First Law — trust is earned through observable evidence; Synthetic Approval activates only after the skill has demonstrated it understands the customer's preferences through a minimum history of correct predictions); C-001 (human override is unconditional — the retrospective override right ensures Synthetic Approval never removes the customer's ultimate authority); GENESIS Part 05 — Skill operating model requires that approval burden scales inversely with demonstrated trust.
Depends On:      C-001 (human override unconditional), C-002 (trust through evidence), C-003 (authority is licensed), C-010 (evidence state machine)
Produces:        DP-015 (Synthetic Approval as learned delegation), AD-017 (Synthetic Approval Confidence Gate)
Contradicted By: NONE — Synthetic Approval is an extension of, not a contradiction to, the approval model. It requires MORE evidence infrastructure than explicit approval, not less.
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  This claim emerges from a fundamental insight about how trust works in professional relationships. A skilled agency team member who has worked with a client for 6 months does not send every routine post for approval — they have learned what the client would approve and act accordingly, while keeping their client informed. The current WAOOAW model (APPROVAL_GATE / PRE_AUTHORIZED) has no mechanism for this earned autonomy on execution. Synthetic Approval fills that gap constitutionally: it is not skipping approval, it is the skill acting as a learned delegate of the customer's stated preferences. Three constitutional safeguards make this valid: (1) the evidence record explicitly marks every synthetic approval as SYNTHETIC — no hidden approvals; (2) the confidence score and evidential basis are recorded — the skill cannot fake certainty; (3) the retrospective override is unconditional — the customer can undo any synthetic approval within the configured override window. This is WAOOAW's USP in the approval model: most AI agents either ask for everything (friction) or ask for nothing (risk). Synthetic Approval is the constitutionally governed middle path. Implementation constraint: the minimum history threshold and confidence threshold are customer-configurable at the skill level. Default minimum history: 20 prior manually approved actions of the same type. Default confidence threshold: 90%. The skill may NOT self-activate Synthetic Approval mode — mode activation requires an explicit Decision Space amendment (C-003 Second Law: authority expansion requires a new licensing event).
