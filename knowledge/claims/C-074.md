Type:            OBLIGATION + PLATFORM_INTEGRITY
Statement:       When a new customer is onboarded with a business domain that requires an MCP capability not currently provisioned, the platform must attempt to provision it automatically without human intervention where credentials are available, and must inform the customer within 1 hour of onboarding with an honest capability status and a guaranteed 24-hour completion SLA for any gap. The platform must never silently degrade capability without disclosure. If Founder action is genuinely required (missing platform credentials), a Founder Action item must be created automatically within 15 minutes of detection, a notification sent to the active steward via Steward Assistant, and the customer informed with the specific gap and the 24-hour timeline. Waiting for a developer to manually update deployment configuration before serving a customer is a platform design failure — not an acceptable operating procedure.

Reviewer Notes:  WHY DEPLOYMENT-GATED CAPABILITY IS UNACCEPTABLE:

                 Before C-074: Ramesh signs up as a restaurant customer. DVE identifies
                 he needs zomato-mcp. zomato-mcp is not in docker-compose. Developer must:
                 raise PR → get Founder approval → deploy. This takes days.
                 During that time: Ramesh has paid for a WoW Expert who cannot connect
                 his restaurant to Zomato. Trust is destroyed on day one.

                 After C-074: Ramesh signs up. Domain capability check detects missing
                 zomato-mcp. Platform asks Ramesh for his Zomato credentials (Skill 1b).
                 Ramesh provides them in 10 minutes. oauth-vault stores them.
                 Container Apps provisions zomato-mcp. Ramesh has full capabilities within
                 1 hour. No Founder involvement. No deployment cycle.

                 THE CLASSIFICATION IS THE KEY INSIGHT:
                 Most "missing MCPs" for new customer domains need the CUSTOMER's
                 credentials, not the Founder's. The platform just needs to ask.
                 Only platform-level credentials (GA4 service account, Meta BM)
                 require Founder action — and even those should be caught at FA-021/FA-022
                 time, not at customer onboarding time.
