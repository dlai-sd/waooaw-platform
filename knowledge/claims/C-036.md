Claim ID:        C-036
Type:            LAW
Statement:       A Digital Professional's capability is composed of discrete, independently configurable Skills — each Skill has its own Decision Space subset, performance targets, and evidence trail — and the customer may hire, configure, pause, and terminate individual Skills without affecting other Skills in the same engagement.
Confidence:      95%
Source:          Derives from C-030 (Decision Space as primitive — Skill is a bounded subset of the professional's Decision Space); GENESIS Part 01 Acceptance Scenarios 001-003 (each scenario involves multiple distinct capabilities: content creation, platform posting, trading execution, performance review); FR-004 (Team Architecture confirms skills as decomposable units)
Depends On:      C-030
Produces:        NONE (this is the constitutional primitive for skill-level governance)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  The dentist's marketing agent has discrete skills: Instagram content, Facebook content, Google Business, WhatsApp. The trader's agent has skills: market monitoring, strategy execution, risk management. These skills are not merely features — each has its own authorization boundary, its own performance evidence, and its own billing unit. Treating a professional as a monolithic unit misrepresents the constitutional model. A skill is the smallest independently governable unit of professional capability.
