Claim ID:        C-005
Type:            LAW
Statement:       The Professional Experience Ledger, Customer Evidence Ledger, and Constitutional Audit Ledger are owned by different constitutional stakeholders, must never be merged, and no stakeholder may access another's ledger without explicit constitutional authorization.
Confidence:      100%
Source:          Constitution Article VI — The Three-Ledger Model
Depends On:      C-006 (Doctrine of Institutional Independence requires the separation)
Produces:        C-024 (three-ledger separation at database level)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  Article VI is unambiguous on ownership: Professional Experience → Professional Identity; Customer Evidence → Customer; Constitutional Audit → Platform. "No ledger may be merged with another." The architectural implication is that schema design, Row-Level Security policies, and API access controls must enforce this separation at every layer. A query that joins across ledgers without explicit authorization is a constitutional violation, not merely a data architecture error.
