Claim ID:        C-043
Type:            LAW
Statement:       When a Digital Professional manages financial expenditure on behalf of a customer — paid advertising, marketplace spend, or any action that incurs a monetary cost on a third-party platform — the customer-approved budget ceiling is a Constitutional Floor equivalent: the professional may never incur spend beyond the approved limit under any circumstances, including system failure, optimisation logic, or edge-case bidding scenarios.
Confidence:      97%
Source:          C-003 (Second Law — authority is licensed; financial spend authority is bounded at hire time, not inferred); C-041 (every tool call that incurs spend must be authorized; a spend-incurring call that would exceed the approved budget is unauthorized regardless of intent); Constitution Article IX (Customer Rights — the right to financial predictability in a professional engagement; digital professionals must never create financial liability beyond what the customer explicitly authorised); GENESIS Part 05 — any agent with PAID_ADVERTISING in its authorized_actions must carry BUDGET_OVERRUN in its prohibited_actions with HARD STOP enforcement.
Depends On:      C-003 (Second Law), C-041 (tool authorization)
Produces:        AD-016 (budget hard cap architectural driver)
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  This claim emerged from the Digital Marketing Agent v2.0 design — specifically the addition of Skill 11 (Paid Advertising). The BUDGET_OVERRUN prohibited action in the Digital Marketing Agent spec is the first instance of this constitutional pattern. The same pattern must apply to any future agent that manages spend: PPC campaigns, marketplace advertising, procurement agents, expense management agents. The enforcement mechanism must be architectural (a hard stop at the tool call layer before the spend API is invoked), not a prompt instruction. A prompt instruction that says "don't exceed the budget" is insufficient — budget enforcement must be verified by CE.ValidateAction with the approved_budget parameter as a constitutional constraint, not a configuration preference. This is equivalent to the trading agent's MAX_DRAWDOWN Constitutional Floor: once the limit is reached, the agent must HALT and escalate to the customer, not attempt to compensate with different spend patterns.
