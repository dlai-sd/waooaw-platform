Claim ID:        C-061
Type:            PLATFORM_SAFETY + LEGAL_OBLIGATION
Statement:       Every platform agent that accepts user-uploaded media (image, audio, video) or produces synthesised media must pass a mandatory Content Safety Scan before any media is stored, processed, or delivered to any channel. Content containing CSAM (Child Sexual Abuse Material), sexual violence, hate speech imagery, or sexually explicit material is categorically and unconditionally rejected. CSAM detection triggers immediate session termination, Constitutional Blocker, and mandatory reporting to NCMEC and CBI under POCSO India Act 2012. The Content Safety Scan is a REQUIRED gate — it is never DEGRADABLE. If the content moderation service is unavailable, the upload or generation is blocked (fail-safe, not fail-open).
Confidence:      99%

Reviewer Notes:  C-061 is the platform's legal compliance floor. WAOOAW is an intermediary under
                 IT Rules 2021 the moment it processes user-generated content. The obligations are:
                 
                 1. SCAN BEFORE STORE: No user-uploaded media may be stored in any WAOOAW database,
                    cloud storage, or temporary buffer before passing the Content Safety Scan.
                    The scan result (PASS / FLAGGED_CATEGORY / CSAM_DETECTED) is written to CAL
                    before any storage action. Evidence First (C-023) applies.
                    
                 2. SCAN BEFORE DELIVER: No synthesised/generated media (images from
                    image-generation-mcp, videos from kling-ai-mcp/runway, audio from elevenlabs)
                    may be delivered to any customer channel before passing the Content Safety Scan.
                    
                 3. CSAM IS ABSOLUTE: If any content is classified as CSAM:
                    a. Session terminates immediately (250ms — same priority as Emergency Stop)
                    b. Media is NEVER stored — immediately purged from memory
                    c. Constitutional Blocker raised: CSAM_DETECTED
                    d. Legal notification to NCMEC (National Center for Missing & Exploited Children)
                       and CBI Cyber Crime division as required under POCSO 2012
                    e. Customer account flagged for review
                    f. No automatic restoration of service
                    This sequence is NOT overridable by any customer action, Emergency Stop,
                    or Founder instruction. POCSO reporting is a legal obligation, not a policy choice.
                    
                 4. OTHER FLAGGED CATEGORIES:
                    SEXUAL_EXPLICIT_ADULT: Reject with explanation; customer warned; no storage
                    HATE_SPEECH_IMAGERY:   Reject with explanation; customer warned; incident logged
                    VIOLENCE_GRAPHIC:      Reject; context-reviewed (some business contexts legitimate
                                          e.g., medical — requires explicit domain whitelist)
                    NUDITY_NON_SEXUAL:     Context-reviewed (medical, artistic — domain whitelist)
                    
                 5. SCAN IS REQUIRED (NOT DEGRADABLE):
                    If content-moderation-mcp is unavailable: BLOCK the upload/generation.
                    Do not allow media to proceed without a safety result.
                    WAOOAW cannot claim intermediary liability protection under IT Rules 2021
                    if it knowingly allowed unscanned content. The fail-safe (block when uncertain)
                    is both legally required and constitutionally mandated.
                    
                 6. GENERATED CONTENT MUST ALSO BE SCANNED:
                    AI image generators (Midjourney, Stable Diffusion equivalents, Kling AI) 
                    can produce inappropriate content even from innocuous prompts through model
                    drift or adversarial inputs. Every generated image/video must be scanned
                    BEFORE delivery — not assumed safe because the prompt was safe.
                    
                 API cost note: Azure Content Safety API costs approximately $0.001 per image
                 and $0.0001 per text request. For a typical DMA customer generating 20 content
                 pieces per month, this is < ₹0.20/customer/month — negligible, non-optional.
