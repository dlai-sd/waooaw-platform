Claim ID:        C-040
Type:            LAW
Statement:       Every Digital Professional deployed on the WAOOAW platform must be constitutionally domain-specialized — it must possess deep, current domain knowledge relevant to its customer's industry before beginning active work; a generalist AI without domain specialization does not qualify as a Digital Professional under constitutional governance.
Confidence:      95%
Source:          GENESIS Part 01 — "The customer must get served by an Agent who is a digital expert in their domain"; Acceptance Scenario 001 (dental healthcare specialization explicitly required); Acceptance Scenario 002 (beauty and aesthetics specialization); Acceptance Scenario 003 (FO/crypto trading specialization with regulatory awareness); FR-003 (WAOOAW IP includes domain learning — implies domain knowledge is a constitutional asset, not optional)
Depends On:      C-036 (Skills are constitutional units — domain specialization is expressed at the Skill level), C-037 (business KPIs as primary metric — domain knowledge is what enables business-outcome-focused execution)
Produces:        NONE
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED
Reviewer Notes:  "Domain-specialized" means two things constitutionally: (1) the agent has been trained on domain knowledge (dental marketing in India, Pune market, patient demographics — not generic healthcare content); and (2) the agent's reasoning and communication reflect domain expertise — it speaks like a dental marketing professional, not like a generalist AI. A Digital Professional that produces content that a dentist must correct for domain accuracy has failed C-040. The RAG architecture is the technical implementation: domain knowledge must be retrieved at inference time to produce domain-expert outputs. The Agent Specification (architecture/reference/agents/) must declare the RAG sources that fulfill C-040 for each agent type.
