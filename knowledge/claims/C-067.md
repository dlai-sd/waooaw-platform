Claim ID:        C-067
Type:            PLATFORM_INTEGRITY + COST_GOVERNANCE
Statement:       All production and staging environment deployments for the WAOOAW platform must use a Blue-Green revision strategy. The active (blue) revision must remain available and serving traffic until the new (green) revision passes all Constitutional Compliance Tests and post-deployment verification. The old revision must be deactivated (scaled to zero) within 30 minutes of the green revision being confirmed healthy, to prevent sustained double-cost. No deployment strategy may allow simultaneous active serving by both revisions for longer than the verification window. Additionally, no deployment may be initiated if the current calendar month's Azure infrastructure spend has exceeded 95% of the constitutional cost ceiling (INR 10,000 per environment per month for dev/qa/demo/uat environments; INR 15,000 per month for production).
Confidence:      99%
Source:          GENESIS Part 01 — Cost Constraint ("dev ~INR 5-6k/month; QA/Demo/UAT each ~INR 6-8k/month; well within 10k limit"); ADR-027 (Cloud Architecture Optimization — 10 cost optimizations); C-001 (Human Override — zero-downtime deployment is the architectural expression of continuous availability; a deployment that takes the service down violates the spirit of C-001); C-065 (SDLC Separation — deployment confirmer verifies green is healthy before blue is deactivated).
Depends On:      C-001 (availability during deployment), C-065 (post-deploy verification before switchover), ADR-027 (cost bounds)
Produces:        Blue-Green deployment script (`scripts/blue-green-deploy.sh`); cost gate in promote.yaml; Azure Container Apps revision management standard
Contradicted By: NONE
Status:          RATIFIED — 2026-07-18 (Founder authorization)
Reviewer Notes:  WHY BLUE-GREEN IS COST-NEUTRAL ON AZURE CONTAINER APPS:

                 Azure Container Apps Consumption plan pricing:
                 - You pay per CPU-second + memory-second USED (not provisioned)
                 - A replica at 0 requests costs ZERO (scales to zero = no charge)
                 - During the 15-30 minute deployment window:
                   Blue (old): receiving 90% → 0% traffic
                   Green (new): receiving 10% → 100% traffic
                 - Both revisions run briefly, then blue is deactivated
                 - Extra cost per deployment: ~₹0.50 (15 minutes × 2 services × 0.5 CPU)
                 - This is constitutionally negligible

                 THE DEPLOYMENT WINDOW COST MATH:
                 5 services × 0.5 CPU × 0.5 Gi × 30 min = 0.025 vCPU-hours + 0.0125 GB-hours
                 Azure pricing: ~$0.000024/vCPU-s + ~$0.000003/GB-s
                 = approximately ₹0.30 per deployment
                 There is no cost argument against Blue-Green on Container Apps Consumption.

                 THE COST GATE:
                 Constitutional cost ceiling per environment per month:
                   dev:     INR 10,000/month
                   qa:      INR 10,000/month
                   demo:    INR 10,000/month
                   uat:     INR 10,000/month
                   prod:    INR 15,000/month

                 Gate thresholds:
                   > 80% of ceiling: WARN — create GitHub Issue, notify Sujay
                   > 95% of ceiling: BLOCK — deployment halted until next billing month
                   > 100% of ceiling: EMERGENCY STOP — scale all non-constitutional services to 0

                 THE 30-MINUTE DEACTIVATION RULE:
                 Blue revision must be deactivated within 30 minutes of green being verified.
                 If deactivation does not occur within 30 minutes (e.g., CI timeout),
                 an automated cleanup job fires: `az containerapp revision deactivate`.
                 Running two active revisions beyond 30 minutes is a cost waste that
                 violates the GENESIS cost constraint — it is a constitutional event.

                 BLUE-GREEN IN AZURE CONTAINER APPS:
                 Container Apps natively supports multiple revisions with traffic splitting.
                 No additional infrastructure is required. The strategy uses:
                   az containerapp ingress traffic set --revision-weight
                 to shift traffic from blue to green, then:
                   az containerapp revision deactivate
                 to scale blue to zero.
