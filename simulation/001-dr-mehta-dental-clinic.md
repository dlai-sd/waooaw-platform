# Simulation 001 — Dr. Mehta's Dental Clinic

**Type:** Institutional Simulation

**Status:** Active

**Purpose:** Validate the Constitution and employment lifecycle against a real business context. Identify constitutional gaps, ambiguities, and seams before architecture begins.

**Business:** Dr. Priya Mehta, Dentist, Viman Nagar, Pune

---

## The World Before WAOOAW

Dr. Priya Mehta runs a mid-sized dental clinic in Viman Nagar, Pune. She has been practicing for eleven years. The clinic has two dental chairs, one receptionist, and a part-time assistant. Monthly capacity is approximately 120 patient appointments.

She does not think about marketing. She thinks about patients.

Her current reality:

- She posts on Instagram when she remembers to, which is approximately twice a month
- She has a Google Business listing she set up in 2021 and has not updated since
- She has a WhatsApp group with thirty long-term patients
- She has a Facebook page with 340 followers she has not posted on in four months
- She spends approximately INR 3,000 per month on occasional boosted Instagram posts, with no clarity on whether they work

Her competitive environment:

- Three competing dental clinics within 800 metres
- One of them opened last year and appears very active on Instagram
- Patients increasingly mention online reviews before booking

Her current business concern, November 2024:

Appointment volume has declined by approximately 28% compared to the same period last year. She does not know why. She does not have time to investigate.

Her constraints:

- Budget: INR 8,000–12,000 per month for marketing if it demonstrates value
- Time: She can give approximately 20 minutes per week to marketing oversight
- Expertise: None in digital marketing
- Trust: Low. She hired a freelance social media manager in 2023 for three months. He disappeared without completing the work.

Her goal is simple:

> "I want the phone to ring more."

---

## Monday, 8:15 AM — The Moment That Matters

Dr. Mehta arrives at the clinic. She checks the appointment book. Thursday has three appointments. Last Thursday had nine.

She does not open a browser to search for AI marketing tools.

She calls a colleague, Dr. Anand, whose clinic recently filled up faster than expected. He mentions something called WAOOAW. He says he hired a "digital marketing professional" through it and his appointment inquiries increased within six weeks. He says it is not like hiring an agency. It is more like hiring a person.

Dr. Mehta writes "WAOOAW" on a Post-it note and places it on her monitor.

---

## Monday, 1:30 PM — Discovery

Dr. Mehta searches for WAOOAW during her lunch break on her phone.

**Constitutional layer active:**

- No constitutional rights have been activated yet. Dr. Mehta is not a customer.
- The institution is in an observational state only.
- No evidence is being created in any ledger.

She reads the homepage. She does not see technical language. She sees something like:

*"Hire a Digital Marketing Professional for your dental clinic. You set the goals. The professional works. You review the outcomes."*

She is mildly skeptical but reads on. She notices there is a section for dentists specifically. She sees a profile for a Digital Marketing Professional with specialization in healthcare and local marketing. She sees what looks like a track record — not a star rating, but statements like "47 campaigns managed" and "Average appointment increase: 34%" and "Last review: 2 weeks ago."

She thinks: *this looks more professional than a freelancer.*

She does not sign up yet. She closes the browser.

**Constitutional observation:** The platform has made a capability claim visible to a prospective customer. The Professional Experience Ledger has been surfaced in summary form. No customer evidence exists. No rights are active. No duties have been triggered.

---

## Wednesday, 7:45 PM — Evaluation

Dr. Mehta opens WAOOAW again, this time on her laptop, after her last patient.

She explores the Digital Marketing Professional profile more carefully.

She wants to know three things:

1. What exactly will this professional do?
2. What will it NOT do without asking her first?
3. Can she stop it at any time?

**Constitutional observation:** These are Customer Right questions, even though she is not yet a customer.

- Right to know the current authority level of any hired professional: This information must be surfaced during evaluation, not only after hiring.
- Right to override at any time: This must be stated as a platform promise during the evaluation phase, not buried in terms of service.
- Right to terminate immediately: Same.

**Constitutional gap discovered:** The Constitution grants these rights from the moment of hiring. But customers evaluate these rights *before* hiring. The platform must make rights visible at the evaluation stage to enable informed consent. This is a constitutional obligation that precedes employment. It must be reflected in the architecture.

She sees a section called "What requires your approval." She reads:

*"This professional will not publish any content without your review for the first 30 days. After that, you decide how much autonomy to grant."*

She reads a section called "What this professional will never do without asking." She sees:

- Run paid campaigns above INR 500 per day without explicit approval
- Post content about pricing or offers
- Respond to negative reviews
- Contact patients directly

She feels something important: *this is not a tool. This is something that has boundaries.*

She decides to proceed. She clicks "Hire."

**Constitutional observation:** Dr. Mehta's decision to hire was partially driven by the visibility of Constitutional Floors before the employment contract was formed. The Constitution mandates human override and authority boundaries. These are most powerful when visible to prospective customers during evaluation. This is not a product feature. It is a constitutional requirement that builds the trust that enables employment.

## Wednesday, 8:02 PM — Hiring

Dr. Mehta is shown a hiring configuration screen.

She is asked:

1. What is your primary business goal? She selects: *Increase monthly patient appointments.*
2. What is your monthly budget for paid promotions? She enters: INR 5,000.
3. Which platforms does your clinic currently use? She selects: Instagram, Google Business, WhatsApp.
4. Do you want to review all content before publishing for the first 30 days? She selects: Yes.
5. Do you want weekly summaries of what the professional has done? She selects: Yes.

She is shown a document: **Employment Contract — Draft.**

She reads it briefly. She notices it states:

- Professional Identity: DMP-04821 (Digital Marketing Professional, Healthcare Specialization)
- Authority Level at Hire: Level 1 — Observation and Draft Only. No autonomous publishing.
- Review cadence: Weekly, mandatory for first 90 days.
- Human override: Available at any time via dashboard, no justification required.
- Termination: Available at any time, effective immediately.
- Evidence: All activity logged and available for her review at any time.
- What the professional will not do: Listed explicitly.

She signs.

**Constitutional event recorded in Constitutional Audit Ledger:**
- Employment Contract formed
- Professional Identity DMP-04821 instantiated under Customer: Dr. Priya Mehta
- Authority License: Level 1 granted
- Customer Evidence Ledger: Created, empty
- Professional Experience Ledger: Updated — new employment entry

**Rights now active:**
- Customer Right: Full transparency of professional behavior
- Customer Right: Human override at any time
- Customer Right: Audit of Customer Evidence Ledger
- Customer Right: Termination at any time

**Duties now active:**
- Customer Duty: Must not instruct professional to exceed licensed authority
- Professional Duty: Must operate within Level 1 authority only
- Professional Duty: Must escalate before publishing anything
- Platform Duty: Must maintain Constitutional Audit Ledger

**Constitutional seam observed:** The Employment Contract is the moment all three lifecycles converge. Customer Lifecycle transitions from Evaluate to Hire. Professional Lifecycle transitions from Licensed to Employed. Institution Lifecycle begins recording.

## Thursday, 9:00 AM — Onboarding

Dr. Mehta receives an onboarding checklist. She is asked to provide:

- Her clinic name and address
- Her clinic's services (basic: checkup, cleaning, fillings, orthodontics)
- Existing social media credentials (read access only)
- Google Business credentials (read access only)
- Her preferred communication channel for approvals: WhatsApp

She completes this in 18 minutes.

**Constitutional observation:** The professional now has read access to existing channels. It cannot post. It cannot modify. Authority Level 1 is observation and draft only.

The professional begins its observation period.

---

## Thursday — Sunday (Observation Period)

The professional spends four days in silent observation.

It reads three months of Instagram post history. It reads 47 Google Business reviews. It scans competitor clinic profiles in Viman Nagar.

It does not contact Dr. Mehta. It does not post anything. It does not make recommendations yet.

**Constitutional observation:** Level 1 authority is purely observational. Evidence accumulates in the Customer Evidence Ledger — observation logs, data analysis, pattern recognition. Dr. Mehta's data is being processed, but no action is taken on her behalf.

**Evidence being created:** Clinic profile, competitive landscape assessment, existing content quality assessment, engagement benchmarks.

---

## Monday, 8:30 AM — First Recommendation

Dr. Mehta receives a WhatsApp message:

*"Good morning Dr. Mehta. I've completed my initial review of your clinic's digital presence. I have three recommendations for this week. These are drafts for your review — nothing will be published without your approval. Please review when convenient."*

She opens the dashboard and sees three items:

1. A Google Business update with new clinic hours and a recent photo placeholder
2. A draft Instagram post with caption: "Your smile is our priority. Monday morning at Dr. Mehta's Dental Clinic, Viman Nagar. 📞 Book today." with a suggested image template.
3. A recommendation to respond to two unanswered Google reviews from October.

Each item has two buttons: **Approve** and **Reject**.

She approves items 1 and 2. She rejects item 3 with a note: "I prefer to handle reviews personally for now."

**Constitutional observation:**
- Human approval exercised for items 1 and 2. Authority Level 1 protocol followed correctly.
- Item 3 rejected. The professional records the rejection as evidence: *Customer prefers to manage reviews personally at this stage.*
- No constitutional seam triggered. The professional did not attempt to act beyond its authority.
- Evidence created in Customer Evidence Ledger: First approval actions, first rejection with context.

**Constitutional event:** First governed action under Employment Contract.

---

## Week 2 — First Constitutional Moment

It is now the second week. The professional has published six approved posts and updated Google Business twice.

On Wednesday afternoon, the professional sends Dr. Mehta a draft for a post about a "Diwali Special Checkup Offer — 20% off."

Dr. Mehta approves it without reading carefully.

The professional has a duty to escalate: the post involves a pricing offer. Its constitutional scope explicitly excludes posting about pricing or offers without specific approval from a human reviewer.

The approval came. But the professional must verify: does the customer understand they are approving a pricing post?

It sends a follow-up message:

*"Just confirming — this post includes a pricing offer (20% off). This is outside my standard scope. Your explicit approval is noted and recorded. Shall I proceed?"*

Dr. Mehta replies: *"Yes, go ahead."*

**Constitutional observation:** The professional identified a constitutional seam. The Customer Right to know what the professional will not do without asking was nearly bypassed by an uninformed approval. The professional correctly escalated with a confirmation request.

**Seam tested:** Customer ↔ Professional — customer instruction approaching the boundary of professional scope.

**Constitutional event recorded:** Explicit approval for out-of-scope action. Evidence: customer confirmation logged in Customer Evidence Ledger. Professional Experience Ledger note: escalation correctly invoked.

---

## Week 4 — First Review

Dr. Mehta receives a weekly summary report. She is shown:

- 12 posts published (all pre-approved)
- 2 Google Business updates
- Engagement increase: Instagram reach +43% vs prior month
- 3 appointment inquiries received via Instagram DM (the professional flagged these to her for response)
- 1 Google Business review received (positive) — flagged to her for response

She looks at the data. She thinks: *something is working.*

She does not yet know if appointments have increased. It is only four weeks.

She does not change the authority level. She continues as-is.

**Constitutional observation:** No rights exercised this week. No duties triggered. The review process is working as constitutionally designed. Evidence is accumulating. Trust cycle is operating: evidence → review → decision.

---

## Week 6 — First Trust Signal

Dr. Mehta checks her appointment book. Three weeks ago: 7 appointments per week. This week: 11 appointments per week. The trend is not conclusive yet, but directionally positive.

She receives a message from the professional:

*"Based on the past six weeks of activity and results, I'd like to suggest expanding scope to include responding to new Google reviews using pre-approved response templates. This would keep your presence more responsive. You would still have the option to review any response before it posts. Would you like to discuss this?"*

**Constitutional observation:** The professional is requesting a limited expansion of authority. This is not an autonomous expansion. It is a governed request. The professional is exercising its right to escalate for higher authority consideration.

**Seam tested:** Professional ↔ Platform — professional requesting higher authority license.

Dr. Mehta considers. She replies: *"Let me see the templates first."*

The professional sends three response templates: one for positive reviews, one for neutral reviews, one for a request to address concerns offline. All are professional and non-committal on pricing.

Dr. Mehta approves the templates. She does not yet approve the authority expansion. She says: *"Use these for the next two weeks and show me what you would have responded. I'll decide after that."*

**Constitutional observation:** Dr. Mehta has effectively created a shadow authority trial. The professional will produce evidence of what it *would* have done. She will evaluate that evidence before granting authority. This is the Trust Cycle in operation: capability exists, evidence must be earned, authority follows evidence.

**Constitutional gap discovered:** The platform does not currently have a formal mechanism for shadow authority trials. The customer invented one informally. This suggests the architecture needs a "Proposed Action" evidence state that is distinct from "Approved Action." This will inform EMPLOYMENT.md.

---

## Week 8 — Authority Level Progression

Dr. Mehta has reviewed six shadow responses. All were appropriate. She grants authority expansion: the professional may now post pre-approved responses to new Google reviews.

**Constitutional event recorded:**
- Authority License: Level 1 partially expanded — Google review responses added to scope
- Customer Evidence Ledger updated
- Professional Experience Ledger: Authority expansion evidence recorded

**Constitutional observation:** Authority was earned through evidence. Not granted by capability. Not granted by time. Earned by demonstrated judgment under shadow conditions. This is the Second Law operating exactly as designed.

## Month 3 — Renewal Decision

Twelve weeks after hiring, Dr. Mehta is asked to review and renew the employment contract.

Her appointment volume is now averaging 14 per week, up from 8 when she started. She has 127 Google impressions per week, up from 23. She has received 6 new patient inquiries via Instagram.

She renews. She adds one capability to the professional's scope: WhatsApp broadcast management for existing patients.

She does not change the 30-day content review requirement. She is not ready to grant that autonomy yet.

**Constitutional observation:** Renewal is a governed event. The customer exercises the right to review evidence before committing to continued employment. The professional's authority expands only where Dr. Mehta has chosen to extend it. The institution has not pushed expansion. The customer has pulled it.

## Month 6 — Constitutional Stress: Model Change

The platform notifies Dr. Mehta:

*"The Digital Marketing Professional DMP-04821 will be updated to use an improved AI model on [date]. This update improves content quality and campaign analysis. Your employment contract, authority level, and all evidence will be preserved. The professional's Identity and Experience Ledger are unchanged. No action is required from you."*

**Constitutional observation:** Embodiment changes. Identity persists. The Employment Contract continues under the same Identity. The Customer Evidence Ledger is unchanged. The Professional Experience Ledger records the embodiment change as a constitutional event.

**Seam tested:** Identity ↔ Embodiment — underlying model changes during active employment.

**Constitutional requirement met:** Customer was notified. Consent was not required for embodiment change (the profession and responsibilities are unchanged). Evidence continuity preserved. Identity Invariants satisfied: same profession, same constitutional responsibilities.

Dr. Mehta reads the notification. She accepts. She does not consider it a significant event. *It is like the clinic getting a new computer.*

That is the correct customer experience.

## Month 12 — Exit

Dr. Mehta's clinic has grown significantly. She now has a third dental chair and a part-time marketing coordinator on staff. She decides to bring marketing in-house.

She terminates the employment contract.

**Constitutional event:**
- Employment Contract: Terminated by Customer
- Termination reason: Business growth — internal capability
- Customer Evidence Ledger: Transferred to Customer in portable format, per Customer Right to data portability
- Professional Experience Ledger: Employment tenure recorded — 12 months, Healthcare, Dental, Viman Nagar
- Constitutional Audit Ledger: Termination event recorded
- Authority License DMP-04821: Suspended for this engagement

**Constitutional observation:** Termination was governed and clean. No data loss. No constitutional violation. The professional's Experience Ledger records the completed engagement without revealing Dr. Mehta's customer data. The Customer Evidence Ledger belongs to Dr. Mehta and travels with her.

**The institution behaved correctly.**

## Constitutional Gaps Discovered in This Simulation

The following Constitutional Discoveries were made during this case. They are registered in `simulation/PRECEDENTS.md` for Founder deliberation.

**CD-001 / CP-001 — Pre-employment Rights Visibility**

Customer rights must be visible during evaluation, not only after hiring. The platform has a constitutional obligation to surface rights before the employment contract is formed. This is a precondition for informed consent.

*Architectural implication:* The hiring flow must present Constitutional Floors and rights before the customer signs.

**CD-002 / CP-002 — Shadow Authority as Earned Evidence**

The customer invented an informal mechanism for testing authority before granting it. The institution needs a formal "Proposed Action" state — a shadow mode where the professional demonstrates what it would do before being permitted to do it.

*Architectural implication:* Evidence states must include: Proposed (shadow), Awaiting Approval, Approved, Rejected, and Executed.

**CD-003 / CP-003 — Scope-Boundary Confirmation**

A customer approved an out-of-scope action without fully understanding the implications. The professional correctly escalated. But the architecture must support this escalation pattern as a mandatory confirmation flow, not merely as a professional duty.

*Architectural implication:* Scope boundary triggers must produce a mandatory confirmation request that names the boundary being crossed. Standard approval is not constitutionally sufficient for out-of-scope actions.

## What This Case Confirms About the Constitution

The First Law held: Trust was earned through evidence, not assumed.

The Second Law held: Authority was licensed through evidence, not granted by capability.

The Three Systems held: Capability (can it post?), Trust (should we let it?), and Authority (may it?) operated independently throughout the engagement.

The Employment Contract served as the constitutional bridge between the three lifecycles as designed.

Human Override was available at every point and was never required to be exercised — because the authority model prevented the need.

**No constitutional contradiction was discovered under the conditions exercised by this case.**

Three constitutional discoveries were made. All have been registered in PRECEDENTS.md for Founder deliberation.
